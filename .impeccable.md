## Design Context

### Users
Cross-cultural Christian leaders: missionaries, expat leaders, multicultural team managers, global church planters. They are purposeful, time-pressed, and internationally mobile. They use this platform to grow as leaders and equip their teams. Context of use: intentional study sessions, often desktop but mobile must work. State of mind when arriving: motivated but discerning — they've seen too many generic Christian resource sites and need to feel this is worth their time and trust.

**Two user types:**
- **Personal pathway user**: Individual leader investing in their own growth. Solo, introspective, wants clear progress and relevant content.
- **Team pathway user**: Team leader managing a cross-cultural team. Needs to see their team, assign content, and track group engagement. Organisational mindset.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **Grounded. Sharp. Global.**

- Warm but not soft — authority without arrogance
- Distinctly Christian without being churchy or preachy — faith is woven in, not performed
- Globally-minded — avoids Western-centric assumptions in language and imagery
- Professional enough to attract organisational leaders, accessible enough for field workers

Tagline: "Cross-Cultural Leadership. Grounded in Faith."

Emotional goal: users should feel *equipped and trusted*, not sold to.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme:** Light — these are professionals using this during work/study hours. Trust, legibility, and credibility come first. The navy hero sections create drama without going full dark mode.

**Visual tone:** Editorial meets mission-organisation. Think a well-designed leadership journal or a premium NGO annual report — not a tech SaaS, not a megachurch website. Clean layouts with enough white space to breathe. Navy and off-white as the dominant surfaces; orange used sparingly for moments of energy.

**Anti-references:**
- NOT: generic Christian ministry sites with clip-art globe icons and rainbow banners
- NOT: tech SaaS with gradients and glowing CTAs
- NOT: Western megachurch aesthetic (too American, excludes the global audience)
- NOT: person-heavy (no Chris photos driving the layout — brand forward)

**References to aim for:** High-quality NGO / international organisation websites, premium leadership publication landing pages.

### Design Principles
1. **Brand-forward, not person-forward** — the platform identity must stand alone without relying on Chris's photo or story
2. **Two pathways clearly distinguished** — Personal and Team pathways must be immediately scannable and feel meaningfully different, not just relabelled versions of the same page
3. **Trust over conversion pressure** — no aggressive pop-ups, countdown timers, or pushy sales patterns. Let the content quality do the selling.
4. **Global visual language** — abstract, geometric, or typographic design elements over stock photos of people (hard to represent all cultures fairly)
5. **Paid content earns its gate** — free content is genuinely useful. The paywall should feel like an invitation to go deeper, not a wall.

### Technical Constraints
- Next.js (App Router) on Vercel
- Supabase for auth and database
- Stripe for payments and subscriptions
- Mobile-first responsive — many users are in the field on phones
- WCAG AA accessibility minimum
